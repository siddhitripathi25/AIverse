Prompt Engineering

Learn to communicate effectively with AI models and get precise, high-quality outputs every time.

Prompt Engineering is the art and science of designing inputs that guide large language models (LLMs) to produce desired outputs. It's one of the most valuable skills in the age of generative AI.

Zero-Shot Prompting

Ask the model to perform a task without any examples. Works well for straightforward requests like summarization, translation, or question answering.

Few-Shot Prompting

Provide a few examples of input-output pairs before your actual request. This helps the model understand the pattern and format you expect.

Chain-of-Thought

Encourage the model to reason step by step before giving a final answer. Dramatically improves performance on math, logic, and complex reasoning tasks.

Role Prompting

Assign a persona or expertise to the model: "You are an expert Python developer..." This shapes tone, depth, and perspective of responses.

Prompt Engineering Best Practices

  • Be specific and clear about what you want
  • Provide context and constraints (format, length, tone)
  • Use delimiters to separate sections of your prompt
  • Iterate and refine prompts based on outputs
  • Test prompts across different models and temperatures
# Example: Chain-of-Thought prompt
prompt = """
Solve this step by step:
A store has 45 apples. They sell 60% in the morning
and receive a delivery of 30 more. How many apples
are left at the end of the day?

Think through each step before giving the final answer.
"""
