Artificial Intelligence: A Modern Approach

Book Stuart Russell & Peter Norvig Beginner

Artificial Intelligence: A Modern Approach (AIMA) is the undisputed standard textbook for Artificial Intelligence. Used in over 1,500 universities across 135 countries, it offers the most comprehensive, up-to-date introduction to the theory and practice of artificial intelligence.

About this Book

This textbook presents AI as the study of agents that receive percepts from the environment and perform actions. It covers classical search, planning, knowledge representation, probabilistic reasoning, machine learning, neural networks, computer vision, natural language processing, and robotics, all unified under a single agent-based framework.

Key Topics Covered

  • Intelligent Agents & Problem-solving (Search, Games)
  • Knowledge, Reasoning, and Planning (Logic)
  • Uncertain Knowledge and Probabilistic Reasoning
  • Machine Learning & Deep Neural Networks
  • Perception (Computer Vision) and Language Processing (NLP)
  • Philosophical, Ethical, and Safety considerations of AI

Why this book is useful

It provides an incredibly rigorous foundation. Rather than teaching just how to use current AI libraries, it teaches the mathematical, logical, and algorithmic underpinnings of the entire AI field. The unified intelligent agent perspective helps tie together very different subfields of AI into a coherent whole.

Who should read it

Undergraduate or graduate computer science students, researchers, and professional developers who want a deep, academic-level understanding of AI. It does require comfort with basic probability, linear algebra, and programming, making it ideal for those who want to go beyond basic tutorials.
