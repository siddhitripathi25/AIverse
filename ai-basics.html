AI Basics

Build a solid foundation in Artificial Intelligence — understand what AI is, how it works, and where it's headed.

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is the simulation of human intelligence in machines programmed to think, learn, and solve problems. From voice assistants to self-driving cars, AI is transforming every aspect of our lives.

History of AI

AI research began in the 1950s when Alan Turing proposed the Turing Test. Key milestones include the Dartmouth Conference (1956), expert systems in the 1980s, and the deep learning revolution starting in 2012.

Types of AI

AI is categorized into Narrow AI (task-specific), General AI (human-level intelligence), and Super AI (beyond human capability). Today, all practical AI systems are Narrow AI.

How AI Works

AI systems learn patterns from data using algorithms. They process inputs, identify features, make predictions, and improve over time through feedback loops and training.

Real-World Applications

AI powers recommendation systems (Netflix, Spotify), medical diagnosis, fraud detection, autonomous vehicles, language translation, and intelligent chatbots.
