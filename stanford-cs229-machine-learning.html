    Stanford CS229: ML Course

    Course Andrew Ng (Stanford University) Free

    Stanford CS229: Machine Learning is a legendary graduate-level course offered by Stanford University. Taught by Andrew Ng, it provides a highly rigorous mathematical treatment of machine learning theory, algorithms, statistical bounds, and applications.

    About this Course

    CS229 covers machine learning topics in extensive mathematical detail. The course is divided into supervised learning (generative/discriminative algorithms, SVMs, neural networks), unsupervised learning (clustering, dimensionality reduction, EM algorithm), learning theory (VC dimension, bias/variance bounds), and reinforcement learning (MDPs, Q-learning, policy search).

    Key Topics Covered

    • Linear/Logistic Regression, Newton's Method, and GLMs
    • Generative Learning (GDA, Naive Bayes) & SVMs (Kernels, Duality)
    • Learning Theory: Bias/Variance, VC Dimension, ERM
    • Unsupervised Learning: EM Algorithm, PCA, ICA, Factor Analysis
    • Reinforcement Learning: MDPs, Value Iteration, Policy Iteration, Q-Learning
    • Practical ML Advice: Error Analysis, Debugging Learning Algorithms

    Why this course is useful

    It bridges the gap between applying models and understanding their mathematical foundation. By deriving algorithms from first-principles, you build the knowledge necessary to modify loss functions, design novel architectures, and conduct advanced research in machine learning.

    Who should take it

    Advanced computer science students, data scientists, and ML researchers who want to go deep into the mathematics of machine learning. Strong prerequisites in linear algebra, multivariable calculus, and basic probability are highly recommended.

    Access Stanford CS229 Materials

    Stanford University provides the course syllabus, lecture videos (available on YouTube), lecture notes, problem sets, and syllabus slides for free on their official portal.
